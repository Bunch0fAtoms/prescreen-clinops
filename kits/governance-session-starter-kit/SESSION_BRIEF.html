3 How the build works

This is a starter kit, not a finished solution. The setup is already in place, so your time goes straight to the policy logic, the part that teaches the platform what to protect. Look for the # TODO (you build this) markers.

✅ Pre-built for you
  • The six shared synthetic OMOP foundation tables, the same tables every group works from. Governing them here is the point.
  • The PHI column map and the governed-tag vocabulary.
  • A verification harness that proves a policy changed what a user sees, owner view versus masked view.
🧠 You build the core
  • The masking logic that decides who sees person_id and who sees ***MASKED***.
  • The row-filter logic that gates patient rows by OCDO group.
  • The identifier search and the inactive-user audit.

Set policy high, once

You classify and set policy at the catalog and schema level, on the shared foundation. That is the point of governance: your masks, row filters, and tag-based policy protect the data every group uses. Because the policy lives high in the hierarchy, it flows down to every new table the other groups build, with no per-table rework.

4 The common thread in action

Built on the foundation first, applied as the others build

Governance is not a separate two-day track. It is the layer under all four sessions.

Governance runs through everything

One policy, set high, inherited everywhere

It starts in the Day 1 whole-room Genie discovery session. As the room asks real questions of the data, you see which columns identify a patient and who should see what. That is your requirements list, gathered live. Then, still on Day 1, you classify the shared foundation and set a tag-based policy at the catalog and schema level. You set it once, high in the hierarchy. On Day 2, as the Data Engineering and Applied AI groups compose new silver tables, gold tables, and a registered model in that same catalog, the policy already covers them. Any new column that carries a PHI tag is masked the moment it appears, with nothing to re-apply.

5 Ground rules

Provable, scoped, synthetic

A security-first customer. These rules are the same across all four sessions.

Everything is provable

Every mask, filter, tag, and audit shows its own before and after. When you are responsible for real patient data, being able to prove a control works is what the work demands.

Governed tag vocabulary

This metastore enforces governed tag policies. phi and data_sensitivity only accept values from an allowed list. A typo is rejected by Unity Catalog, which is the point.

Unity-Catalog-scoped

Catalog, schema, and group come from widgets. No hive_metastore, ever. No hardcoded secrets.

Synthetic data only

No real PHI in this kit. The controls are identical when Fred Hutch points them at real curated_omop later.
